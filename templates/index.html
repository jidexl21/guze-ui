🍏 What is FSA Compliance?

The Food Standards Agency (FSA) is the government body responsible for food safety and hygiene across England, Wales, and Northern Ireland.

For Nigerian food imports to reach UK retail shelves, they must comply with:

  • Correct UK food labelling standards (ingredient listing, allergens, weights)
  • Safe transport and storage temperature chains
  • Traceability records from origin to point of sale
  • Microbiological safety standards for high-risk categories
  • Pesticide maximum residue levels (MRLs)

GUZE pre-screens all food products against FSA requirements before they are listed on the platform.

Visit FSA Website ↗
⚖️ What is OPSS Compliance?

The Office for Product Safety & Standards (OPSS) is the UK's national product safety regulator, sitting within the Department for Business & Trade.

For non-food consumer goods (cosmetics, textiles, electronics, etc.) imported from Nigeria, OPSS requires:

  • UK Conformity Assessment (UKCA) marking where applicable
  • Appointment of a UK Responsible Person (UKRP)
  • UK lab test reports from accredited testing facilities
  • Safety data sheets and technical documentation
  • Registration on the UK product safety database

Every supplier on GUZE must submit and have approved their UKRP assignment and lab test reports before listing products.

Visit OPSS Website ↗
✅ What is DCTS Duty Free?

The UK's Developing Countries Trading Scheme (DCTS) provides preferential tariff rates for imports from developing nations — including Nigeria.

Under DCTS, many Nigerian goods qualify for:

  • Zero-rated import duty on eligible product categories
  • Simplified customs procedures for qualifying goods
  • Reduced tariffs on textiles, food, handcrafts, and cosmetics

GUZE verifies DCTS eligibility for every product using the UK Global Tariff database and manages certificates of origin on your behalf.

This is one of the primary cost advantages of importing Nigerian goods through our platform vs. third-country routes.
